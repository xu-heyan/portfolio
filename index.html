PROJECT 1
VR + App Interface Design Real - Virtual - Real
Design Brief

Digital inclusion and social participation are getting more and more attention by the society.

Among the marginal groups, the social participation of social anxiety disorder patients is low, they suffers in many social occasions.

We hoped to explore and develop a design solution with digital and interactive technologies to fosters digital inclusion and social participation with social anxiety disorder group.

We found through research that public speaking is the most fearful occasion for this group. Our design focuses on helping them improve their symptoms in that situation.

The Biggest Challenge

Our first concept is high-tech glasses. After wearing them, the faces of the audience will be replaced by the faces of people users are familiar with or cartoon characters users like.

However, through the communication with our tutor, we found that the concept is not solving problems, but helping users to avoid problems. So we abandoned this concept.

After searching for a lot of traditional treatments, I incorporated a lot of traditional treatments that have proven effective in the new concept, such as Exposure Therapy, Performance Feedback Treatment. Therefore, the new concept would be more practical and effective.

Result

We finally produced an effective, easy-to-use high fidelity prototype

Reflection

In the design of high-tech solutions, it is not enough to look for existing high-tech or digital solutions. The traditional solutions are worth learning. When designing applications, adding game elements and reward mechanism can promote user participation and improve user experience.

Design Brief

Through the following decades, placemaking has become a cornerstone of urban policy thinking.

The design of public space, through lighting technology or screen, shows the story shared by people in the community, creates a place that people can relate to, experience and enjoy.

The 2019–20 coronavirus pandemic made people stay at home for a much longer time.

I designed the space on top of a car park in my community, hoping to articulate shared stories, promote community communication and ease lonelinessin in times of social hardship.

The Biggest Challenge

It was very difficult to realize multi-user interaction when the users use TouchOSC. I need to distinguish where each received message comes from.

There is no answer to this problem on Google. In addition, after checking all the methods of oscMassages on the official website(Schlegel, 2011)，I found that there was no such method to view the IP address of the mobile device that is sending messages.

In order to solve this problem, I checked the source code of oscmessage in the oscp5 library, and supplemented the relevant java knowledge, so I understood the concept of inheritance. I found that oscmessage inherits the oscpacket class and found the method to get IP address from oscpacket class.

Result

I came up with a complete program, and used keystone to simulate the effect of the design being projected on the site.

Reflection

In public space design, paying attention to the characteristics of the site itself can make the design more interesting. For example, I chose the flat area on top of the car park, so I chose the theme related to the car and created a kind of 3d illusion that people can see the inside of the car park through a hole in the car park roof.

PROJECT 3
Multiplayer Web Interactive Game Thin Out The Exhaust
Design Brief

In the past decade, the use of cars has increased rapidly. Pollutants such as CO, HC, NOx, and PM in vehicle exhaust emissions can aggravate China's air pollution, which will affect people's living environment and health.

Many cities have implemented policies such as restricting vehicles on alternate days and giving priority to the development of public transport to encourage citizens to drive less.

My last work conveys the environment-friendly idea of drive less and walk more, but its users were limited to residents in two buildings. I hope to convey this idea to more people in a fun way in my new project.

The Biggest Challenge

About the communication between mobile and PC: At first, I used the OSC technology in the previous project and changed the oscp5 library to the osc.js library. However, it requires that all participants must be on the same WiFi or connected to the same VPN, and users need to download and configure TouchOSC app. These preparations are too cumbersome.

In order to solve this problem, I searched many technologies, and finally decided to use websocket technology to realize the communication only between web pages, and it doesn’t need users to be in the same network.

My server built by node.js will send different (sender or display) web files according to the client device (mobile or PC). How the system works is shown in right figure.

The Biggest Challenge

It was not easy to make a 3D sphere consist of stars. First, I used complex formulas to figure out where each star should be in 3D space, but such a program is complex, and made the computer hot. To solve this problem, I checked some artists' works on OpenProcessing. It doesn't need 3D renderer to achieve 3D effects. I refered to shimotake's method. He achieved the effect of a sphere being made of small dots with just 2d renderer.

Result

I came up with a a static website where worldwide users can interact with it

Reflection

I learned from the project that providing customization and adding interactivity makes users feel freer and improves their user experience.
